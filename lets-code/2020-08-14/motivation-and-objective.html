Let's Code! - 2020-08-14

Motivation and objective

I am a self taught programmer and have been earning a living as a freelancer or running a small software company for some 20 years. I also love to teach and find ways to simplify complex subjects. And I've had an interest in teaching programming for some time. My ultimate goal is to teach those that might want to earn a living as computer programmers or use their newly found skills to launch their own software ideas. But before I can do that I have to develop my skills teaching the subject and polish the concepts that I have in mind. And that's where you come in.

Real world programming

Far too many times to be a coincidence I've worked with college graduates that were simply not ready for real world programming. I can't really explain it. All I can say is that the vast, if not all, of what I've learned has been by reading and then applying what I've read. I learned by doing. That is what I'd like to offer the group, the opportunity to learn by doing or seeing how it is done in the real world. We won't spend too much time with theory or discussing how things work behind the scenes. I ultimately want this to be about getting things done.

Now, I realize this is a very diverse group. And your reasons for joining the group might not perfectly align with my ultimate goals. That's okay. All I want to do at this time is learn how to best teach some of the concepts that I have in mind. And I appreciate your help in that regard.

When and How

Our first live meeting will be Monday the 24th at 7:30 pm Eastern Daylight Time (-4 GMT). I plan on having up to two sessions of no more than an hour and a half per week (the other will likely be Sunday nights but I'm open to suggestions). My goal is for each session to conclude with a working program. We may do the same program several times in different ways and with various degrees of quality. So it is okay if you miss a session here and there. And yes, we will eventually get to build a Zoom like app.

I will likely record each session so that I can review my performance and see where I can improve. I may also make them available online for others to learn from. Please private message me to discuss if this is an issue and I'll try to address any concern that you may have.

To be clear, this is not a course per se. It does not have a fixed end date. After all, I'm still learning about programming myself - even after all this time.
