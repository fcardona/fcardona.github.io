Industry terms vs programming languages

I believe that a big part of learning comes from understanding the definitions of terms. And every profession or industry has their own jargon. For example, the word beam has different meanings depending on the industry. For a carpenter it is what supports a roof or floor. For a sailor it is the width of a ship or boat. We witnesses also have our own language. Service to us is much different than service for someone in the military.

Today I realized that learning a programming language is just part of the story, you will need to learn industry terms. As I share industry terms, bear in mind that you will be introduced to words that currently have one meaning for you and a different meaning in the programming industry.

Here is one such term: console ( listen to how is pronounced )
What does that mean to you?

If you are on a Windows computer press the Windows key on your keyboard (or click on the Windows icon on the lower left), type console, and then the enter key. What program opens up?
Now, do the same thing but instead of console type terminal. The same program should open up.

Console has had many definitions over the years. For our purposes, console, or terminal, is a text based interface that allows us to communicate (or give commands) to the computer.
