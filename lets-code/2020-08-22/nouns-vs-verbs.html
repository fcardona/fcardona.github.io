Nouns vs verbs

Previously I mentioned how learning a programming language can be compared to learning a foreign language. One thing that all natural languages have are nouns and verbs. Today I thought I'd briefly review some ways nouns and verbs are expressed in the programming language that we will be using on Monday, JavaScript.

Nouns are persons, places, or things. You have already been introduced to several common nouns in the industry such as the word console. You may have also noticed several nouns in your HTML webpage: title, body, image, etc.

Verbs are action words. These are actions taken by nouns or actions taken upon nouns. In the sentence "Felix wrote a letter" we have two nouns and one verb.

We programmers try to _model_ our code based on real world nouns and verbs used by our customers. Imagine you were asked to create an app to track a publisher's activity in the field ministry.
